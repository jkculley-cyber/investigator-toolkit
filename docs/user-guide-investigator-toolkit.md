**Campus Investigation Management**

Version 1.0 | Clear Path Education Group, LLC
Support: support@clearpathedgroup.com

---

## Quick Start

1. **Open** the Investigator Toolkit in your browser.
2. **Enter your information** in Settings: district name, campus, your name, and school year.
3. Click **New Case** to start an investigation.
4. Select the **offense type** (fighting, drugs, threats, harassment, or general misconduct).
5. The toolkit auto-generates a **due process checklist** and starts your **10-day compliance timer**.
6. Work through the investigation pipeline: Intake > Open > Conference > Decision > Disposition > Closed.

---

## 1. Overview

The Investigator Toolkit helps campus administrators (APs, principals) manage student discipline investigations from intake through disposition. It provides structured workflows, compliance tracking, evidence management, and due process documentation -- all stored privately on your device.

### Privacy First

All data is stored **on your device only**. No data is sent to any server (except a one-time license key verification). Your investigation records never leave your computer unless you explicitly export them.

---

## 2. Settings

Before creating your first case, configure your settings:

1. Click **Settings** in the navigation.
2. Enter your **district name** and **campus name**.
3. Enter your **name** (appears on exported reports).
4. Set the **school year** (e.g., 2025-2026).
5. The **case counter** auto-increments. You can reset it at the start of each school year.

---

## 3. Creating a Case

1. Click **New Case**.
2. Select the **offense type**:

| Offense Type | Texas Education Code Reference |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Fighting | TEC 37.001(a)(1) -- Conduct Code violations |
| Drugs | TEC 37.006 -- Mandatory DAEP removal offenses |
| Threats | TEC 37.001(a)(2) -- Threatening behavior |
| Harassment | TEC 37.001(a)(4) -- Harassment per district policy |
| General Misconduct | TEC 37.001 -- General Student Code of Conduct violations |

3. Enter the **student name**, **date of incident**, and **initial narrative**.
4. Click **Create**. The case enters the **Intake** stage.

---

## 4. Investigation Pipeline

Cases move through six stages, displayed as a **kanban board** on the main screen.

### Stage Descriptions

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Intake** | Initial report received. Gather preliminary information. Assign investigator. |
| **Open** | Active investigation. Collect evidence, interview witnesses, document findings. |
| **Conference** | Student/parent conference held. Present findings, hear student's account. |
| **Decision** | Administrator makes disciplinary decision based on evidence and conference. |
| **Disposition** | Final action recorded (consequence assigned, referral made, case resolved). |
| **Closed** | Investigation complete. All documentation finalized. |

### Moving Cases

- Click a case card to open the detail view.
- Click **Advance to Next Stage** to move the case forward.
- You can also move cases backward if you need to revisit a stage.

## 5. 10-Day Compliance Tracker

Texas Education Code requires timely processing of discipline investigations.

- The toolkit tracks **business days** (school days) from the incident date.
- A countdown badge appears on each case card.
- At **day 7**, the badge turns yellow (warning).
- At **day 10**, the badge turns red (overdue).

### SPED/504 Alerts

If you mark a student as having a **SPED IEP** or **504 plan**:

- The toolkit displays a prominent alert: **MDR (Manifestation Determination Review) required within 10 school days**.
- For 504 students: **504 coordinator must be notified**.
- These alerts persist until you acknowledge them in the due process checklist.

## 6. Case Detail -- 10 Sections

Each case has ten organized sections accessible from the case detail view:

1. **Case Summary**: Student info, offense type, dates, current stage, compliance timer.
2. **Incident Narrative**: Detailed description of what happened.
3. **Evidence Log**: Upload or describe physical evidence, screenshots, video references, documents.
4. **Witness Statements**: Record statements from witnesses (students, staff, others).
5. **Parent Contact Log**: Document all parent/guardian communications with dates and outcomes.
6. **Due Process Checklist**: 8 auto-generated steps (see Section 7).
7. **Conference Notes**: Record the student/parent conference discussion and outcomes.
8. **Decision & Rationale**: Document the disciplinary decision and reasoning.
9. **Appeal Tracker**: Track any appeals filed, hearing dates, and outcomes.
10. **Threat Assessment Log**: For threat-related cases, document the threat assessment process and findings.

---

## 7. Due Process Checklist

When you create a case, the toolkit auto-generates an **8-step due process checklist**:

1. Student notified of charges
2. Student given opportunity to respond
3. Parent/guardian notified
4. Evidence collected and documented
5. Student/parent conference scheduled
6. Conference held -- student's account heard
7. Decision communicated to student and parent in writing
8. Appeal rights communicated

Check off each step as you complete it. Incomplete steps appear as warnings when you try to advance to the Decision or Disposition stage.

## 8. Evidence Log

For each case, maintain a detailed evidence record:

1. Open the case and go to the **Evidence Log** section.
2. Click **Add Evidence**.
3. Enter a **description** of the evidence item.
4. Select the **type** (document, photo, video, statement, digital, physical, other).
5. Add **notes** about where the evidence was obtained and its relevance.
6. Click **Save**.

Evidence items are listed chronologically and included in PDF exports.

---

## 9. Parent Contact Log

Document every parent/guardian interaction:

1. Open the case and go to **Parent Contact Log**.
2. Click **Add Contact**.
3. Record the **date**, **method** (phone, email, in-person, letter), **who was contacted**, and **outcome**.
4. Click **Save**.

A thorough contact log strengthens your due process documentation.

---

## 10. Appeal Tracker

If a student or parent files an appeal:

1. Go to the **Appeal Tracker** section.
2. Click **Add Appeal**.
3. Enter the **date filed**, **grounds for appeal**, and **hearing date** (if scheduled).
4. Update the **status** as the appeal progresses (Filed, Hearing Scheduled, Heard, Upheld, Overturned).
5. Document the **outcome**.

The **Trends** page provides analytics across all your cases:

- SPED/504 case percentage.
- Monthly case volume trends.

## 12. Exports

### PDF Export (Per Case)

1. Open the case detail view.
2. Click **Export PDF**.
3. A formatted PDF is generated with all 10 sections, including the due process checklist and evidence log.

### ZIP Bundle Export

1. From the main case list, select multiple cases.
2. Click **Export ZIP**.
3. Each case is exported as an individual PDF, bundled into a single ZIP file.

### CSV Export

1. From the main case list, click **Export CSV**.
2. A spreadsheet-compatible file is downloaded with case summary data.

### JSON Backup & Restore

Since all data is on-device, use JSON backup for safekeeping.

- **Backup**: Go to **Settings > Export Backup**. Save the JSON file securely.
- **Restore**: Go to **Settings > Import Backup**. Select a previously saved JSON file.

---

## 13. Tips for Effective Investigations

- **Start the checklist immediately.** The 10-day clock starts on the incident date, not when you open the case.
- **Log parent contacts in real time.** Do not wait until the case is closing to document calls and emails.
- **Check SPED/504 status early.** The MDR timeline is strict. Verify IEP/504 status during Intake.
- **Use the conference notes section.** A well-documented conference protects the district in appeals.
- **Back up regularly.** Your data lives on your device. Export a JSON backup at least weekly.
