# Navigator User Guide

**ISS/OSS Referral & Placement Management**

Version 1.0 | Clear Path Education Group, LLC
Support: support@clearpathedgroup.com

---

## Quick Start

1. **Log in** to your Waypoint account. Navigator appears in the sidebar under the blue Navigator section header.
2. Open the **Navigator Dashboard** to see active referrals, placements, and risk summaries.
3. To create a new referral, click **New Referral**.
4. Fill in the student, referral reason, and supporting details. Click **Submit**.
5. An administrator reviews and approves the referral, then creates a placement (ISS, OSS, or other).

---

## 1. Overview

Navigator manages In-School Suspension (ISS), Out-of-School Suspension (OSS), and other short-term discipline placements. It provides referral tracking, support service coordination, risk scoring, and equity analytics -- all integrated with the Waypoint platform.

### Who Uses Navigator

| Role | Primary Actions |
|------|----------------|
| Teacher / Counselor | Submit referrals for students |
| AP / Principal | Review referrals, approve/deny, create placements |
| Admin | Full oversight, configure goals, view district-wide analytics |
| Counselor | Assign supports, track progress, manage skill gaps |

---

## 2. Referrals

### Creating a Referral

1. Click **New Referral** from the Navigator Dashboard or sidebar.
2. Select the **student**.
3. Choose the **referral reason** (behavior category).
4. Add a **narrative** describing the incident or pattern.
5. Optionally attach supporting documentation.
6. Click **Submit**.

### Reviewing a Referral

Administrators and principals see pending referrals on the Dashboard.

1. Click the referral to open the detail view.
2. Review the student's history, risk score, and any prior referrals.
3. Choose **Approve** or **Deny**.
4. If approved, proceed to create a placement.

---

## 3. Placements

### Creating a Placement

After approving a referral:

1. Click **Create Placement** from the referral detail page.
2. Select the **placement type**: ISS, OSS, or Other.
3. Set the **start date** and **end date**.
4. Assign a **location** (for ISS) or document the notification (for OSS).
5. Record **parent notification** details.
6. Click **Save**.

### Tracking Active Placements

- The Dashboard shows all active placements with days remaining.
- Overdue placements (past end date but not closed) are highlighted.
- Click any placement to view details, add notes, or close it.

---

## 4. Support Services

Supports are interventions assigned to students during or after a placement.

1. Open a student's placement or referral.
2. Click **Add Support**.
3. Select the **support type** (counseling, mentoring, behavior plan, academic support, etc.).
4. Assign a **staff member** responsible for delivering the support.
5. Set the **start and end dates**.
6. Track progress with status updates.

---

## 5. Risk Scoring

Navigator assigns each student a **risk score from 0 to 100** based on:

- Number of referrals (recent and historical)
- Severity of offenses
- Frequency and recency patterns
- Prior placement outcomes

### How to Use Risk Scores

- **0-30 (Low)**: Standard interventions.
- **31-60 (Moderate)**: Targeted supports recommended.
- **61-100 (High)**: Intensive intervention and monitoring. Escalation engine may trigger automatic actions.

Risk scores update automatically as new data enters the system.

---

## 6. Campus Goals & Progress

Administrators can set campus-level goals for Navigator metrics.

1. Go to **Navigator > Goals**.
2. Click **New Goal**.
3. Select the **campus**, **metric** (e.g., reduce ISS referrals by 10%), and **target date**.
4. Progress is tracked automatically against live data.
5. The Dashboard displays goal progress with visual indicators.

---

## 7. Escalation Engine

The escalation engine automates responses based on configurable rules.

- **Example**: If a student receives 3 referrals in 30 days, automatically notify the counselor and create a support plan.
- **Example**: If a student's risk score exceeds 75, flag for administrator review.

Escalation rules are configured by district administrators in **Navigator > Settings > Escalation Rules**.

---

## 8. Skill Gap Map

The skill gap map identifies behavioral and social-emotional skill deficits across students.

- View skill gaps by **student**, **campus**, or **district**.
- Common skill categories: conflict resolution, impulse control, emotional regulation, peer relationships.
- Use skill gap data to target support services and group interventions.

Access the skill gap map from **Navigator > Skill Gaps**.

---

## 9. Effectiveness Tracking

Track whether placements and supports are producing positive outcomes.

- **Recidivism rate**: Percentage of students with repeat referrals after placement.
- **Support completion rate**: Percentage of assigned supports completed.
- **Outcome trends**: Improvement or decline over time.

View effectiveness data from **Navigator > Effectiveness**.

---

## 10. Disproportionality Radar

Navigator monitors discipline data for disproportionate impact across student demographics.

- View referral and placement rates by **race/ethnicity**, **gender**, **SPED status**, and **economic status**.
- Statistical indicators flag groups that are over- or under-represented relative to enrollment.
- Use this data for equity audits and policy review.

Access from **Navigator > Disproportionality**.

---

## 11. Reports & Data Import

### Reports

- Filter by campus, date range, placement type, referral reason, or student demographics.
- Export to **PDF** or **Excel**.

### Data Import

- Import referral or student data via **CSV upload**.
- Use the column mapping wizard to align your data with Navigator fields.
